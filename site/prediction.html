Factors used by the model

Four base factors form each driver's Power Rating (weights sum to 100%). Two per-race multipliers then adjust it for the specific circuit, and a simulation shock adds season-long uncertainty. Source badges show real API data vs curated estimates.

Form REAL DATA
Base rating weight 38% Race results 2024–26
Recency-weighted championship points per race. 2026 counts most; older seasons fade and within a season weight halves every 6 races.
Qualifying pace REAL DATA
Base rating weight 19% Qualifying / grid 2024–26
Recency-weighted average grid position — one-lap speed, separate from race pace.
Reliability REAL DATA
Base rating weight 14% DNFs in results 2024–26
1 − DNF rate (weighted). Also sets each car's per-race retirement chance in the sim.
Car index MIXED
Base rating weight 29% Constructor standings + curated package
80% live 2026 constructor strength, 20% curated package (downforce + straight-line).
Circuit fit CURATED EST.
Per-race × Curated car ↔ circuit traits
Per-race ×0.88–1.12. Matches the car's downforce, straight-line speed, tyre management, weight and over/understeer balance to the circuit's demands (curves vs straights, tyre stress).
Track history REAL DATA
Per-race × Driver's finishes at this circuit 2024–26
Per-race ×0.88–1.12 from the driver's own average finishing position at that specific circuit. Strong history boosts; weak history penalises; none = neutral.
Weather (wet pace) MIXED
Per-race × Open-Meteo forecast + curated wet skill
If rain is forecast for the next race, a curated driver wet-weather skill swings the odds — strong wet drivers (e.g. Verstappen, Hamilton, Alonso) gain, weaker ones lose. Scales with the rain probability; no effect on a dry forecast.
Form uncertainty MODEL
Simulation Per-simulation random shock
Each of the 20k simulated seasons draws a constant per-driver strength shock (upgrades, slumps) so the points leader isn't a near-certainty.

How the numbers are produced

For each driver a Power Rating is the weighted sum of the four base factors above. For every one of the 15 remaining rounds that rating is multiplied by circuit fit and track history to get a per-race strength. A Plackett–Luce / Gumbel simulator then runs the whole rest of the season 20,000 times — sampling a finishing order each race (plus retirements and a season-long form shock) — and counts how often each driver wins the next race and the title.

Recency weighting: 2026 races count ×1.00, 2025 ×0.55, 2024 ×0.25; within a season weight halves every 6 races. Retirement chance per race = (1 − reliability). Season form shock σ = 1.6.

⚠️ Educational / entertainment model. Real data (results, qualifying, reliability, standings) drives the ratings; car downforce, weight and handling balance are curated approximations (no public API exposes them) and are labelled as estimates throughout.
